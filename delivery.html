Service delivery policy

Everything I deliver is digital. Nothing ships in a box, and your inbox is the delivery address.

Last updated: July 11, 2026

I provide software engineering services. Everything is delivered digitally. There is no physical shipment, no couriers, and nothing is posted to any address. This page explains how delivery works, how long it takes, and what to do if something does not arrive.

How delivery works

  • During the project, you get access to a staging URL where you can follow progress, and you receive regular updates.
  • On completion, I hand over the git repository and deploy the work to your infrastructure.
  • Credentials and documentation are sent to your email as part of the handover.

Delivery timelines

Timelines are stated in the written quote for your project and measured from the date the advance payment is received. Typical ranges are:

  • Web applications: 3 to 6 weeks.
  • SaaS MVPs: 6 to 10 weeks.
  • APIs: 2 to 4 weeks.
  • Websites: 1 to 2 weeks.
  • Care plans: ongoing, delivered monthly.

What counts as delivered

Work is considered delivered once it is deployed and accepted against the scope agreed in your written quote.

Delays

Delays are usually caused by pending input or approvals from your side, such as content, credentials or third party accounts. If a delay of any kind arises, on my side or yours, I communicate it proactively and give you a revised date rather than letting a deadline pass silently.

Post delivery support

Every project includes a 30 day bug fix window from delivery, as described in the terms and conditions.
